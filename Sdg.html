Sustainable Development Goals

Introduction

The Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs) are a set of 17 global goals established by the United Nations to create a better world for everyone by 2030. Below are three important SDGs:

1. No Poverty

The aim is to end poverty in all its forms everywhere.

Learn more about No Poverty

2. Quality Education

This goal ensures inclusive and equitable quality education and promotes lifelong learning opportunities for all.

Learn more about Quality Education

3. Climate Action

Take urgent action to combat climate change and its impacts.

Learn more about Climate Action
